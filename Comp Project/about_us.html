History of Escuela Catolica De San Sebastian, Inc.

THE INSTITUTION

1996

1996

The establishment of Escuela Catolica de San Sebastian, Inc. as a parochial school of the Parish of San Sebastian in Pinagbuhatan, Pasig City in 1996 has truly been a grace-laden realization of a holy aspiration to institutionalize Jesus' Messianic Office of Teaching within the context of shepherding thrust of the Parish of San Sebastian. No less than Divine Providence could amply cause a poor, newly erected parish to cherish a vision, possess the determination and arduously realize what is now known as Escuela Catolica de San Sebastian.

'96 - '06

1996 - 2006

In 1996, there were only two Kinder Classes. In 1997 a section of Grade I Class was added up: then a section each of Grades II and III in 1998, a section of Grade IV in 1999, a section of Grade V in 2000, a section of Grade VI in 2001, a section of First Year in 2002, a section of Second and Third Year High School in the School Year 2003 to 2005 and a section of Fourth Year in the School Year 2005 to 2006. There has been no let up in the protracted endeavor to cope with the developmental needs of the school plant and its facilities. From. a two-room apartment, ECSS is now a landmark in Pinagbuhatan, Pasig with a four-storey building of 14 classrooms.

The faithful Catholics of San Sebastian Parish, under the persevering leadership of Rev. Fr. David C. Colong., have been principally instrumental in the historic establishment of a Catholic Educational Institution, known as Escuela Catolica de San Sebastian, in the Parish Pinagbuhatan Pasig City, Fr. Colong was Priest of San Sebastian during the time it was built.

2003

2003

In 2003, Rev. Fr. Ronnie M. Samaniego, a very young and energetic Diocesan priest, was assigned as parish priest and at the same time the Director of ECSS, The same year was a witness to the improvement of the school. The kindergarten room was renovated. The library, clinic and even the canteen were improved. It was during this year when the office of the Director and the Principal were constructed.

2004

2004

In October 2004, Most Rev. Bishop Francisco C. San Diego of the Diocese of Pasig assigned Rev. Fr. Elpidio M. Geneta as the 3rd Parish Priest of San Sebastian Parish and School Director of ECSS. Fr. Geneta continued the legacy of his predecessors and continued to improve the school facilities like the construction of new Administration Office, two additional rooms for High School, and making the Pre-School classroom fully airconditioned. He also formed the Drum and Lyre, and fully supported the various clubs and organizations in the school. It was also during this year that the school was granted government recognition for Nursery/ Kindergarten Course and for complete Elementary Course.

2006

2006

In November 2006, Rev. Fr. Emmanuel C. Hipolito was assigned as the new Parish Priest and School Director of ECSS. He continued to implement the planned activities of his predecessor. He remained steadfast in his conviction of promoting integral evangelization by developing catholic discipleship, academic excellence and social justice among faculty, staff, pupils and students. More so, on the core of his leadership, faculty and staff were trained to become competent and efficient educators thru various seminars and other faculty development activities.

2008

2008

In 2008, the school received government recognition for the complete Secondary Course.

2013

2013

In 2013, the school tied up with PCMed, a technology provider/company to provide quality computer education for the 21st century learners. In the same year, the Audio-visual Room (AVR) was inaugurated for educational purpose. The room is equipped with flat-screen TV and LCD Projector for viewing of subject-related films and for serving as venue to other school-related activities.

2014

2014

In 2014, the Science & Technology Curriculum was improved through the integration of the Robotics Program. Said enrichment program aims to promote the technological advancement of pupils/students

2015

2015

On January 12, 2015, the school was granted Certification Status by the Fund for Assistance to Private Education (FAPE). The same certification continues as the school was granted re-certification on March 1, 2019. This means that ECSS passed the Private Education Assistance Committee's (PEAC) Certification and Re-Certification Program. Qualified students receive government educational subsidy and preferably graduate of elementary public schools.

'15 -'21

2015-2021

From 2015-2021, the improvement of the physical facilities took a big leap under the directorship of Rev. Fr. Orlindo F. Ordona. Improvement of infrastructure included efficient electrical wiring and service from the Meralco hence all classrooms and the faculty room became fully air-conditioned. In addition, the school building was retrofitted because it was considered necessary for stable flooring and protection from the impact of a possible earthquake. The façade of the school, the canteen as well as the comfort rooms were improved. Likewise, a planned building for the Senior High School has been designed. Personnel welfare was equally addressed foremost of which were on spiritual and professional growth. Medical support was started and continues to date. On September 29, 2017, the school was accepted member of the Catholic Educational Association of the Philippines (CEAP). On June 1, 2021, Rev. Fr. Daniel L. Estacio took over as the Director. With his leadership, we look forward to the continuous progress of the school both at the academic and non-academic aspects.

Inc'

Incorporation

Aside from her integration with the Pasig Diocesan Schools System (PaDSS), Manila Ecclesiastical Province School Systems Association (MAPSA) Catholic Educational Association of the Philippines (CEAP), ECSS has been registered as a non-stock and non-profit educational corporation with the Securities and Exchange Commission since July 11, 1997.

Patron and Official Seal

The first Catholic Missionary Evangelizers landed on the shores of Pinagbuhatan on the 20th of January 1572. It was the feast day of St. Sebastian. A mass, commemorating his feast day, was celebrated for the first time in the whole of Pasig. St. Sebastian has subsequently become the Patron Saint of Pinagbuhatan. It is but proper that he should be enthroned as the Patron Saint of the school. St. Sebastian most generously shed his blood in fidelity to his Savior Jesus Christ.

Patron and Official Seal

The Official seal of Escuela Catolica de San Sebastian is a red circle on a white background. Inside the circle are people looking up to the Cross of Jesus Christ and the Image of Patron Martyr, St. Sebastian, fenced in by an olive branch on both sides sprouting from the Book of Holy Scriptures.

2021+

2021-Present

Red, the color of "blood" is the official color of ECSS. It stands for martyrologic courage to uphold her principles and her faith, The red circle symbolizes the embeddedness of ECSS in the eternal value of love as embodied in the person of Jesus Christ, by word (symbolized by Holy Scriptures) and by deed (symbolized by the Cross) and emulated by the Patron St. Sebastian (represented by his image). The person symbolizes the ECSS children who aspire to approximate the enshrined ideal of life. The measure of their success would be the measure of peace and harmony (symbolized by the olive branches) that would secure their lives. The white color of the background is the symbol of ECSS's sincere determination to achieve her aspiration- harmonious ordination of her children's lives towards becoming loving witnesses of the Lord Jesus Christ by word and by deed according to the example of St. Sebastian for all eternity.
